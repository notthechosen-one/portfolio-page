Puerto Galera

   A town in in the island of Oriental Mindoro

    A tourist hotspot perfect for doing activities like diving, swimming, kayaking, paddleboarding in the water. For land activities   
  tourist can hike, visit the villages of the Mangyan or eat foods that are popular in the town. This town has many more to offer,
  you can visit the town by riding the ferry from Batangas City.

   This is one of the many islands in the Philippines that is a must to go to. In the Pre-Colonial Era, Puerto Galera is a place
  where all of the indigenous Mangyan people lived. When the Spanish arrived it became the safe harbour for trading to Manila
  and other parts of Asia. As they continue to be occupied by Americans and Japanese, they finally gained independence and
  making the island a place for tourists to visit and checkout traditional food and what they do.

   It is my favorite place because it has many beaches and swimming pools which is my favorite because I enjoy swimming.
  The town also has cuisines that is not very common to me, exploring different food choices, whether it is based on tradition or
  modern innovation, it was truly a delightful adventure. Every meal was an opportunity to experience new flavors and the culinary expertise of the area, from
  savory seafood meals grilled fresh by the seaside to unusual local specialties I had never heard of before. It added another
  level of cultural immersion to my trip and really enhanced my time in Puerto Galera.
